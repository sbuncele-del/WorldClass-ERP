## 2. Services

### 2.1 Service Provision

2.1.1 Provider shall provide the Service to Customer in accordance with:
- This Agreement
- Applicable Orders
- The Service Level Agreement
- The Documentation

2.1.2 Provider shall use commercially reasonable efforts to make the Service available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, subject to the Service Level Agreement.

### 2.2 Service Orders

2.2.1 Customer may order Services by executing an Order with Provider.

2.2.2 Each Order shall specify:
- The Services and modules included
- The number of Authorized Users
- The Subscription Term
- The Fees payable
- Any additional terms

2.2.3 Orders are incorporated into and governed by this Agreement.

### 2.3 Service Modifications

2.3.1 Provider may update, modify, or enhance the Service from time to time.

2.3.2 Provider shall provide reasonable notice of material changes.

2.3.3 Provider shall not materially reduce the functionality of the Service during a Subscription Term without Customer's consent.

### 2.4 Support Services

2.4.1 Provider shall provide support services as specified in the Service Level Agreement.

2.4.2 Support includes:
- Technical assistance via email, chat, and phone (per plan)
- Bug fixes and error corrections
- Access to Documentation
- Software updates and upgrades

## 3. Customer Obligations

### 3.1 Acceptable Use

3.1.1 Customer shall use the Service only for lawful business purposes and in accordance with the Documentation.

3.1.2 Customer shall not:
- Use the Service to process data for third parties without authorization
- Sublicense, resell, or share access to the Service
- Attempt to reverse engineer or decompile the Service
- Introduce malicious code or interfere with the Service
- Exceed the authorized number of Users or usage limits

### 3.2 User Management

3.2.1 Customer is responsible for:
- Managing Authorized User accounts
- Ensuring Users comply with this Agreement
- Maintaining the confidentiality of login credentials
- Promptly deactivating accounts of former employees

3.2.2 Each User account is for a single individual and may not be shared.

### 3.3 Data Responsibilities

3.3.1 Customer is responsible for:
- The accuracy, quality, and legality of Customer Data
- Obtaining necessary consents for personal information
- Maintaining appropriate backups of critical data
- Ensuring Customer Data does not infringe third-party rights

### 3.4 Compliance

3.4.1 Customer shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations in its use of the Service, including:
- Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA)
- Tax laws and SARS requirements
- Industry-specific regulations
- Export control laws

## 4. Fees and Payment

### 4.1 Fees

4.1.1 Customer shall pay the Fees specified in each Order.

4.1.2 All Fees are:
- Quoted in South African Rand (ZAR)
- Exclusive of VAT unless stated otherwise
- Non-refundable except as expressly provided

### 4.2 Invoicing and Payment

4.2.1 Provider shall invoice Customer in accordance with the payment terms in the Order.

4.2.2 Payment is due within 30 days of invoice date unless otherwise specified.

4.2.3 Late payments shall bear interest at 2% per month.

### 4.3 Taxes

4.3.1 Customer is responsible for all applicable taxes except Provider's income taxes.

4.3.2 Provider shall add VAT at the prevailing rate to all invoices.

4.3.3 Customer shall provide valid tax documentation upon request.

### 4.4 Fee Adjustments

4.4.1 Provider may adjust Fees upon renewal with 60 days' notice.

4.4.2 Fee increases shall not exceed 10% annually unless:
- Agreed by Customer, or
- Required by regulatory changes, or
- Exceptional circumstances apply

### 4.5 Disputed Invoices

4.5.1 Customer must notify Provider of any invoice dispute within 14 days.

4.5.2 Undisputed amounts remain payable while disputes are resolved.

6.1.1 Each Party agrees to:
- Protect the other Party's Confidential Information
- Use Confidential Information only for purposes of this Agreement
- Not disclose Confidential Information to third parties except as permitted

6.1.2 Confidential Information includes:
- Customer Data
- Provider's technology and business information
- The terms of this Agreement

### 6.2 Permitted Disclosures

6.2.1 A Party may disclose Confidential Information:
- To employees, contractors, or advisors who need to know, subject to confidentiality obligations
- As required by law (with notice if permitted)
- With the other Party's written consent

### 6.3 Exclusions

6.3.1 Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:
- Is or becomes publicly available without breach
- Was known prior to disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Is received from a third party without confidentiality restrictions

### 6.4 Duration

6.4.1 Confidentiality obligations survive termination for 3 years, except:
- Trade secrets remain protected indefinitely
- Personal information remains protected as required by law

## 7. Data Protection

### 7.1 Data Processing

7.1.1 Provider processes Customer Data as an operator (processor) on Customer's behalf.

7.1.2 Customer remains the responsible party (controller) for Customer Data.

7.1.3 The Data Processing Agreement (Schedule B) governs the processing of Personal Information.

### 7.2 POPIA Compliance

7.2.1 Both Parties shall comply with the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA).

7.2.2 Provider shall:
- Process Personal Information only on Customer's documented instructions
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures
- Notify Customer of data breaches without undue delay
- Assist Customer with data subject requests

### 7.3 Data Location

7.3.1 Customer Data is stored in South Africa (AWS Cape Town region).

7.3.2 Provider shall not transfer Customer Data outside South Africa without Customer's consent, except:
- Where necessary for technical operations
- Where adequate safeguards are in place

### 7.4 Data Security

7.4.1 Provider shall implement and maintain appropriate security measures, including:
- Encryption of data at rest and in transit
- Access controls and authentication
- Regular security assessments
- Incident response procedures

### 7.5 Data Return and Deletion

7.5.1 Upon termination:
- Customer may export Customer Data for 90 days
- After 90 days, Provider shall delete Customer Data
- Provider may retain data as required by law

- Loss of profits, revenue, or business opportunities
- Loss of data (except as provided in Section 7)

- The greater of: (a) Fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim, or (b) R1,000,000

### 9.3 Exceptions

- Breach of confidentiality obligations
- Breach of data protection obligations
- Indemnification obligations

## 11. Term and Termination

### 11.1 Term

11.1.1 This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues until terminated.

11.1.2 Each Subscription has the term specified in the Order and automatically renews unless terminated.

### 11.2 Termination for Convenience

11.2.1 Either Party may terminate a Subscription at renewal by providing notice at least 60 days before the renewal date.

### 11.3 Termination for Cause

11.3.1 Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if:
- The other Party materially breaches and fails to cure within 30 days of notice
- The other Party becomes insolvent, bankrupt, or enters liquidation
- Required by law or regulatory authority

### 11.4 Suspension

11.4.1 Provider may suspend access to the Service if:
- Customer fails to pay Fees when due (after 14 days' notice)
- Customer materially breaches this Agreement
- Required for security or legal compliance

11.4.2 Provider shall provide notice of suspension where practicable.

### 11.5 Effect of Termination

11.5.1 Upon termination:
- Customer's access to the Service shall cease
- Customer shall pay all outstanding Fees
- Customer may export Customer Data for 90 days
- Each Party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information

### 11.6 Survival

11.6.1 The following provisions survive termination: Definitions, Intellectual Property, Confidentiality, Data Protection (as applicable), Warranties and Disclaimers, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, and General Provisions.

## 12. Dispute Resolution

### 12.1 Governing Law

12.1.1 This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Republic of South Africa.

### 12.2 Negotiation

12.2.1 Before initiating formal proceedings, the Parties shall attempt to resolve disputes through good-faith negotiation between designated representatives for 30 days.

### 12.3 Mediation

12.3.1 If negotiation fails, the Parties shall submit the dispute to mediation administered by the Arbitration Foundation of Southern Africa (AFSA).

12.3.2 Mediation shall take place in Johannesburg, South Africa.

### 12.4 Arbitration

12.4.1 If mediation fails, the dispute shall be resolved by arbitration under AFSA rules.

12.4.2 The arbitration shall:
- Be conducted by a single arbitrator
- Take place in Johannesburg, South Africa
- Be conducted in English
- Be confidential

### 12.5 Urgent Relief

12.5.1 Nothing prevents either Party from seeking urgent interim relief from a court of competent jurisdiction.
