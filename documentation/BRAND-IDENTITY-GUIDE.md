# SiyaBusa ERP — Brand Identity Guide

# Part 1: Brand Foundation

## 1.1 Brand Story

### Who We Are

**Masaphokati Technologies (Pty) Ltd** is a South African technology company building enterprise software that empowers African businesses to compete on the world stage.

Our flagship product, **SiyaBusa ERP**, is more than software—it's a statement. The name "SiyaBusa" comes from the Zulu/Nguni word meaning **"We Rule"** or **"We Govern."** This isn't arrogance; it's a declaration of sovereignty over our own destiny, our data, our processes, and our future.

### Our Origin

Born from the reality that African businesses have long been underserved by enterprise software designed elsewhere, for others. We saw organizations forced to adapt to systems that didn't understand their context, their compliance requirements, their way of doing business.

We said: **Enough.**

We built SiyaBusa ERP from the ground up—AI-powered, POPIA-compliant, designed for the unique realities of African enterprise while meeting global standards.

### The Name Meaning

| Language | Word | Meaning |
|----------|------|---------|
| Zulu | SiyaBusa | "We Rule" / "We Govern" |
| Swati | Masaphokati | "Kings" / "Rulers" |

Our company name and product name both carry the same essence: **sovereignty, leadership, and empowerment.**

They walk into any boardroom with quiet confidence—not arrogance, but the self-assurance that comes from knowing their worth. They've earned their seat at the table through excellence, not connections. They speak with authority but listen with humility. They're approachable but not casual. # Part 2: Visual Identity

## 2.1 Logo & Wordmark

### Primary Logo

The SiyaBusa wordmark uses clean, modern typography that conveys:
- **Strength** — Bold letterforms
- **Clarity** — High legibility
- **Modernity** — Contemporary sans-serif

### Logo Usage Rules

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use approved color versions | Create new color variations |
| Maintain clear space | Crowd the logo |
| Use on appropriate backgrounds | Place on busy images |
| Scale proportionally | Stretch or distort |

Minimum clear space around logo = height of the "S" in SiyaBusa

## 2.3 Typography

### Font Families

| Usage | Font | Weight | Fallback |
|-------|------|--------|----------|
| **Headlines** | Segoe UI | Bold (700) | Arial Bold |
| **Subheadings** | Segoe UI | Semi-Bold (600) | Arial |
| **Body Text** | Segoe UI | Regular (400) | Arial |
| **Code/Data** | Consolas | Regular | Courier New |

### Type Scale

| Element | Size | Weight | Case |
|---------|------|--------|------|
| Hero Title | 48-52pt | Bold | Sentence |
| Page Title | 32-36pt | Bold | Sentence |
| Section (H1) | 24-28pt | Bold | Sentence |
| Subsection (H2) | 18-20pt | Semi-Bold | Sentence |
| Body | 11-12pt | Regular | Sentence |
| Caption | 9-10pt | Regular | Sentence |
| Tagline | 14-16pt | Light/Italic | Uppercase |

### Typography Rules

1. **Never use all caps** except for the tagline and short badges
2. **Maintain hierarchy** — readers should scan easily
3. **Generous line height** — 1.5-1.8 for body text
4. **Limit fonts** — use the brand fonts consistently

## 2.4 Imagery Style

### Photography

| ✅ Use | ❌ Avoid |
|--------|---------|
| Real African business settings | Generic stock photos |
| Diverse, professional people | Stereotypical imagery |
| Modern office environments | Outdated technology |
| Clean, well-lit scenes | Cluttered or dark images |
| Authentic moments | Overly staged poses |

### Icons & Illustrations

- **Style**: Line icons, minimal, consistent stroke weight
- **Color**: Primary blue or white on dark backgrounds
- **Approach**: Functional, not decorative

### Graphics

- Clean geometric shapes
- Subtle gradients
- Data visualization: charts, graphs with brand colors
- Avoid: 3D effects, drop shadows, gradients that clash

# Part 3: Voice & Tone

## 3.1 Brand Voice

### The SiyaBusa Voice

Our voice is **confident, clear, and empowering**. We speak as experts who want to share knowledge, not gatekeep it.

### Voice Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Confident** | State facts directly, no hedging | "SiyaBusa handles 10,000+ transactions daily" not "SiyaBusa can potentially handle..." |
| **Clear** | Plain language, no jargon soup | "Track your inventory in real-time" not "Leverage integrated supply chain visibility solutions" |
| **Empowering** | Focus on what customers can achieve | "Take control of your finances" not "We provide financial management" |
| **Respectful** | Professional but warm | "We're here to help" not "Obviously you need to..." |
| **Authoritative** | Knowledgeable but not condescending | Explain without talking down |

### Tone Variations

| Context | Tone Adjustment |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Marketing** | Inspiring, bold, benefit-focused |
| **Technical Docs** | Precise, instructional, helpful |
| **Support** | Patient, reassuring, solution-oriented |
| **Legal/Compliance** | Formal, exact, comprehensive |
| **Social Media** | Conversational, engaging, human |

---

## 3.2 Writing Guidelines

### Do's and Don'ts

| ✅ Do | ❌ Don't |
|-------|---------|
| Use active voice | Use passive voice |
| Be specific | Be vague |
| Focus on benefits | List only features |
| Use "you" and "your" | Use "users" or "customers" |
| Get to the point | Bury the lead |
| Use Oxford comma | Skip the final comma |

### Terminology

| Always Use | Never Use |
|------------|-----------|
| SiyaBusa ERP | Siyabusa, SIYABUSA, Siya Busa |
| Masaphokati Technologies | Masaphokati Tech |
| multi-tenant | multi tenant, multitenant |
| real-time | realtime, real time |
| AI-powered | AI powered, ai-powered |
| POPIA compliant | POPI compliant, POPIA Compliant |

### Headlines & Taglines

- Lead with action or benefit
- Keep under 10 words when possible
- Use the brand voice attributes
- Can use sentence fragments for impact

**Good:**
- "Take Control of Your Business"
- "Enterprise Power. African Soul."
- "Compliance Without Complexity"

**Avoid:**
- "A Really Great ERP Solution for Your Business Needs"
- "We Are the Best ERP in Africa"
- "Buy Our Software"

# Part 4: Applications

## 4.1 Document Standards

### Cover Page Elements

Every formal document includes:

1. **SiyaBusa ERP** logo/wordmark (top)
2. **Document Title** (prominent, uppercase for impact)
3. **Document Category** (subtitle)
4. **Company Name**: Masaphokati Technologies (Pty) Ltd
5. **Metadata Box**:
   - Document Version
   - Effective Date
   - Classification (Public/Internal/Confidential)
   - Compliance badges
6. **Tagline** (bottom, styled as accent)

### Tagline Styling

The tagline "We Rule. We Govern. We Empower." should be:
- **Font**: Light or Regular weight (300-400)
- **Color**: Light Blue `#93c5fd` on dark backgrounds
- **Spacing**: Letter-spacing: 2-3px
- **Case**: Uppercase
- **Position**: Near bottom of cover, with breathing room
- **Treatment**: Subtle, not competing with title

## 4.2 Marketing Collateral

### Pull-Up Banners

**Specifications:**
- Size: 850mm x 2000mm (standard)
- Background: Brand gradient (deep navy to midnight)
- Content hierarchy:
  1. Logo (top, 15% of height)
  2. Hero message (middle, largest text)
  3. Key benefits (3 bullet points max)
  4. Tagline (bottom accent)
  5. Website URL

**Example Layout:**
```
┌────────────────────────┐
│      SiyaBusa ERP      │  ← Logo
│                        │
│   TAKE CONTROL OF      │  ← Hero (largest)
│   YOUR BUSINESS        │
│                        │
│   ✓ 50% Cheaper        │  ← Benefits
│   ✓ Built for Africa   │
│   ✓ AI-Powered         │
│                        │
│  We Rule. We Govern.   │  ← Tagline (light blue)
│     We Empower.        │
│                        │
│     siyabusa.co.za     │  ← URL
└────────────────────────┘
```

**Note:** Banner messaging should be disruptive and action-oriented:
- Lead with the competitive advantage (50% cheaper than SAP/Oracle)
- Emphasize the African identity differentiator
- Technology claims last (AI, modules are expected)

### Business Cards

**Front:**
- Logo centered
- Tagline below

**Back:**
- Name, Title
- Email, Phone
- Website
- QR code to website (optional)

### Presentation Slides

- **Title slide**: Full gradient background, logo, title
- **Content slides**: White background, navy headers
- **Data slides**: Charts in brand colors
- **Closing slide**: Gradient with tagline and contact

# Part 5: Brand Culture

## 5.1 Our Values in Action

### Sovereignty (We Rule)

> We take ownership. We make decisions. We lead.

**In practice:**
- We build solutions, not excuses
- We own our mistakes and fix them
- We set the standard, not follow it
- We control our destiny through excellent work

### Governance (We Govern)

> We bring order, structure, and compliance.

**In practice:**
- We follow processes that work
- We document what we do
- We ensure compliance is built-in, not bolted-on
- We make the complex simple

### Empowerment (We Empower)

> We lift others up. Their success is our success.

**In practice:**
- We train and support without condescension
- We build tools that make people more capable
- We price fairly so quality is accessible
- We share knowledge freely

---

## 5.2 Internal Culture

### How We Work

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| **Excellence over perfection** | Ship quality work; iterate and improve |
| **Clarity over cleverness** | Simple solutions beat complex ones |
| **Ownership over blame** | Fix problems, don't assign fault |
| **Progress over politics** | Results matter more than appearances |
| **Empathy over ego** | Understand before judging |

### Communication Standards

- **Be direct**: Say what you mean
- **Be respectful**: How you say it matters
- **Be constructive**: Critique ideas, not people
- **Be responsive**: Acknowledge, then respond
- **Be documenters**: If it's important, write it down

---

## 5.3 External Relationships

### With Customers

- Treat every customer like a partner
- Under-promise, over-deliver
- Respond quickly, resolve thoroughly
- Celebrate their successes publicly (with permission)

### With Partners

- Clear agreements, mutual benefit
- Transparent communication
- Shared success metrics
- Long-term relationship focus

### With the Market

- Compete on quality, not attacks
- **Never name competitors in public documents**
- Let our work speak for itself
- Build the market up, not tear others down
