## 2. Understanding Your Needs

Based on our discussions, Property Denvour manages a residential property portfolio of 16 occupied units. The following matters require attention for the current financial year-end:

### 2.1 Deposits Incorrectly Classified as Revenue
Tenant deposits received during the financial year have been recorded as revenue. Per accounting standards, deposits held on behalf of tenants are a **liability** (not income) because they are refundable. These need to be reclassified from the Revenue account to an appropriate Liability account (e.g., "Tenant Deposits Held").

**Impact if not corrected:**
- Revenue is overstated
- Liabilities are understated
- Financial statements will not reflect a true and fair view
- Tax implications — you could be paying tax on amounts that are not income

### 2.2 Unallocated Deposit Refunds
Deposit refunds have been processed through the bank but have not been allocated against the corresponding tenant payable balances. These transactions are sitting in the bank feed/register and need to be matched and applied.

**Impact if not corrected:**
- Payables remain overstated for tenants who have already been refunded
- Bank reconciliation may show unexplained differences
- No clear trail of which tenants have been settled

### 2.3 Inactive Account Cleanup
Several tenant accounts are no longer active (tenants have vacated and received refunds) but remain active in the system. These need to be marked inactive to maintain a clean working environment.
