The Plan

Here's What We're Doing

We are restructuring your remuneration package so that your company, Boikago Events & Creatives, pays your children's school fees tax-free through a company bursary scheme. We are also adding a travel allowance to further reduce your personal tax.

✅ Our Advice

Set up a formal company bursary scheme under Paragraph 12B of the Seventh Schedule to the Income Tax Act. Keep your cash salary at or below R600,000 per year. The school fees will then be 100% exempt from tax in your hands — and still deductible as an expense for the company.

Golden Rules

3 Rules — Don't Break These

  1. Company pays the school directly — always

    Every payment must come from the Boikago Events & Creatives company bank account straight to the school. Never pay personally and claim back.

  2. Keep your cash salary at or below R600,000/year

    If your salary goes above R600k, the bursary exemption falls away and the school fees become taxable. If you need more money, take it as dividends (taxed at 20% vs up to 45% for salary).

  3. Fill in the logbook every single day

    No logbook = no travel deduction. SARS will reject it. It takes 30 seconds per trip. Do it daily — never try to fill it in at year-end from memory.

⚠️ If SARS audits you, they will ask for three things: the bursary policy, proof of payment from the company to the school, and the travel logbook. As long as you have these, you are fully protected. This is our job to make sure you're covered — but the logbook is on you.
