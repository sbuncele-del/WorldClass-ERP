## 3. WHAT IS A PBO?

A **Public Benefit Organisation (PBO)** is a non-profit organisation approved by SARS to carry on one or more **public benefit activities (PBAs)** as listed in **Part I of the Ninth Schedule** to the Income Tax Act. PBO approval grants the organisation:

- **Exemption from Income Tax** (Section 10(1)(cN))
- **Exemption from Donations Tax** (Section 56(1)(h))
- **Exemption from Estate Duty** (Section 4(h))
- **Exemption from Transfer Duty** (Section 9(1)(e))
- **Exemption from Skills Development Levy**
- Eligibility to issue **Section 18A Tax Deduction Certificates** to donors (if approved for Part II activities)

## 5. PBO REGISTRATION PROCESS — STEP BY STEP

The PBO registration process with SARS follows the steps outlined below:

### Step 1: Preliminary Assessment (Week 1–2)
- SGBS Group reviews the Organisation's founding document and activities
- Identify which **Public Benefit Activities** (PBAs) under the Ninth Schedule apply
- Determine if amendments to the founding document are required

### Step 2: Founding Document Review & Amendment (Week 2–4)
- Ensure the founding document contains all **mandatory clauses** required by Section 30:
  - A clause confirming the organisation is established solely for the object of carrying on public benefit activities
  - A dissolution/winding-up clause directing remaining assets to another PBO or government
  - A clause prohibiting the distribution of funds to any person (except for reasonable remuneration)
  - A clause requiring that no single person directly or indirectly controls the organisation
  - A clause requiring at least three unconnected persons as trustees/directors/office bearers
  - A clause confirming the organisation will comply with reporting and record-keeping requirements

### Step 3: Gather Supporting Documents (Week 3–5)
- Collect all required documentation (see Section 6 below)
- Ensure documents are certified and current

### Step 4: Complete Application Forms (Week 5–6)
- Complete SARS Form **EI 1** (Application for approval as a PBO — Section 30)
- Complete SARS Form **EI 2** (Application for Section 18A approval — if the Organisation wishes to issue tax-deductible receipts to donors)

### Step 5: Submission to SARS (Week 6–7)
- Submit the complete application pack to:

  **SARS Tax Exemption Unit (TEU)**
  PO Box 436
  Pretoria, 0001

  Or via email: **taboreg@sars.gov.za**

  Or via eFiling (if available)

### Step 6: SARS Review & Processing (Week 7–30+)
- SARS reviews the application and may request additional information or clarification
- The standard processing time is **90 to 180 business days** (approximately 4–9 months), though it can take longer
- SGBS Group will follow up regularly and respond promptly to any SARS queries

### Step 7: Outcome & Approval
- SARS issues a **PBO Reference Number** and an approval letter
- If Section 18A is also approved, the Organisation will be authorised to issue tax-deductible donation receipts

## 8. KEY COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS (POST-APPROVAL)

Once approved as a PBO, the Organisation must:

1. **File annual IT12EI returns** with SARS within 12 months of the financial year-end
2. **Maintain proper books and records** for a minimum of 5 years
3. **Report any changes** to the founding document, activities, or office bearers to SARS within a reasonable time
4. **Not distribute profits or gains** to any person other than in the course of carrying on a public benefit activity
5. **Use funds solely** for the approved public benefit activities
6. **Ensure at least three unconnected persons** serve as office bearers at all times
7. **Issue Section 18A certificates** correctly (if approved) — containing all prescribed details

Non-compliance may result in the **withdrawal of PBO status** by SARS.
