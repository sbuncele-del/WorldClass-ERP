Purpose & Background
Why Kingdom Champions Exists
01
Background
Kingdom Champions is the youth ministry of The Kingdom Embassy, Centurion. It was established to create a structured, consistent and spiritually grounded environment where young people can grow in their faith, develop leadership skills, and build meaningful relationships within the body of Christ. The ministry exists because the church recognises that young people are not the future of the kingdom — they are active participants now, called to reign and take dominion in every sphere of life.
02
Purpose
The purpose of this programme is to provide a clear annual structure for engaging young people through scripture study, fellowship, outdoor bonding, and milestone events. Rather than ad-hoc meetings, Kingdom Champions runs a consistent monthly rhythm — Bible Chat on Zoom, Extended Sunday Sessions in smaller groups, and Outdoor Activities on the last Saturday — so that every young person knows when and where they can show up, grow, and contribute. The programme also includes special events that mark the spiritual calendar of the ministry, including Youth Month (June) and the annual Youth Conference in the Kingdom of Eswatini (December).
03
2026 Theme — Dominion Generation Enthroned
The 2026 theme, “Dominion Generation Enthroned”, is a declaration of identity and mandate. It speaks to a generation of young people who are not waiting for permission to walk in authority — they are already seated with Christ (Ephesians 2:6) and called to exercise dominion in every area of their lives. The year’s formula, Believe It · See It · Receive It, provides a practical spiritual framework: first establish faith, then develop vision, and finally walk in the manifestation.
04
What This Document Covers
This programme lays out the full April–December 2026 calendar, including:
Bible Chats
9 monthly Zoom sessions — scripture-led discussion in breakout groups, anchored by a devotional passage each evening.
Extended Sunday Sessions
9 in-person sessions after Sunday service — youth separate into leader-led groups for discussion and practical application.
Outdoor Activities
8 Saturday outings — hiking, sports, prayer walks and fellowship braais, with church transport and a shared lunch.
Special Events & Conferences
Youth Month (June) — including the Youth Economic Indaba with the Marketplace Council — plus the year-end Eswatini Conference.
