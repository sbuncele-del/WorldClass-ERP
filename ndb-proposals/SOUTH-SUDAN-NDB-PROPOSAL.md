## Executive Summary

This business plan presents the establishment of the **National Development Bank of South Sudan (NDB-SS)**, a specialized development finance institution designed to mobilize investment capital for strategic national infrastructure and economic development projects.

Modeled after successful development finance institutions such as the **Development Bank of Southern Africa (DBSA)**, **African Development Bank (AfDB)**, and **Qatar Development Bank**, NDB-SS will serve as the primary vehicle for channeling both commercial investment and donor funding into transformative projects across South Sudan and the broader African region.

### Seed Capital Request

**We are seeking USD 5,000,000 (Five Million US Dollars) in seed capital** to establish and operationalize the National Development Bank of South Sudan. This seed investment will fund:

- Legal establishment and regulatory licensing
- Initial operations and working capital (18 months lean)
- Essential technology infrastructure and banking systems
- Senior management team recruitment
- First project pipeline development

**This seed capital will enable NDB-SS to mobilize USD 1.2 Billion over 3 years** (USD 400 Million annually) through deal origination, earning approximately **USD 20 Million in commission revenue per year**.

## 2. Market Opportunity

### 2.1 South Sudan Development Context

South Sudan, the world's youngest nation (independence 2011), faces enormous development challenges but also unprecedented opportunities for transformative investment.

**Key Economic Indicators:**

| Indicator | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Population | ~12 million |
| GDP | ~$4.5 billion |
| GDP per Capita | ~$375 |
| Economic Dependence | 95%+ on oil exports |
| Infrastructure Gap | $50+ billion estimated |
| Road Network | <200km paved (entire country) |
| Electrification Rate | <5% |
| Banking Penetration | <10% |

### 2.2 Infrastructure Deficit

**Roads & Transport:**
- Less than 200km of paved roads in a country larger than France
- No functioning railway network
- Limited air transport infrastructure
- River transport underutilized despite extensive Nile system

**Energy:**
- Less than 5% of population has electricity access
- Massive hydropower potential (Nile) untapped
- Solar potential among highest in Africa

**Agriculture:**
- Only 4% of arable land currently cultivated
- Massive irrigation potential from Nile basin
- Food imports despite agricultural potential

### 2.3 Funding Landscape

**Current Development Finance Sources:**

| Source | Annual Flow (Est.) | Limitations |
|--------|-------------------|-------------|
| Humanitarian Aid | $1.5-2 billion | Short-term, fragmented |
| Bilateral Aid | $500 million | Tied to donor priorities |
| Multilateral (World Bank, AfDB) | $300 million | Slow disbursement |
| Private Investment | <$100 million | High risk perception |
| Oil Revenue (Government) | Variable | Volatile, misallocated |

**Gap Analysis:**
- **Required Annual Investment**: $5-10 billion for meaningful development
- **Current Investment**: <$3 billion (mostly humanitarian)
- **Funding Gap**: $2-7 billion annually
- **Development Finance Gap**: No dedicated DFI to structure bankable projects

### 2.4 Regional Opportunity

**East African Community Integration:**
- South Sudan seeking EAC membership
- Regional infrastructure connectivity needed
- Cross-border trade potential with Uganda, Kenya, Ethiopia, DRC

**Pan-African Development Agenda:**
- African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA)
- Programme for Infrastructure Development in Africa (PIDA)
- Agenda 2063

## 3. Business Model

### 3.1 Dual-Track Approach

NDB-SS operates two complementary business streams:

#### Track A: Commercial Development Finance
- Source investment funds for viable, revenue-generating projects
- Structure deals to ensure repayment
- Focus: Infrastructure with commercial returns (toll roads, power, mining)

#### Track B: Donor Fund Coordination
- Attract and manage donor/humanitarian funding
- Channel to development-aligned humanitarian projects
- Focus: Social infrastructure (schools, hospitals, water systems)

### 3.2 Revenue Model

**Primary Revenue Streams:**

| Revenue Source | Description | Rate/Fee |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| **Deal Origination Commission** | Fee for connecting funders to bankable projects | **5% of deal value** |
| **Fund Management Fee** | Annual fee for managing deployed capital | 1-2% AUM |
| **Transaction/Paymaster Fee** | Fee for disbursement management | 0.5-1% of disbursements |
| **Advisory Services** | Project preparation, feasibility studies | Project-based |
| **Success Fees** | Bonus on project completion/milestones | Negotiated |

**Example Revenue Calculation (Year 1):**

| Activity | Volume | Rate | Revenue |
|----------|--------|------|---------|
| Deals Closed | $400 million | 5% | $20,000,000 |
| Paymaster Services | $300 million | 0.75% | $2,250,000 |
| Advisory/Other | Various | - | $2,000,000 |
| **Year 1 Total Revenue** | | | **$24,250,000** |

### 3.3 Operational Model

**Model 1: Deal Connector (Commission Model)**
```
Funder (DFI/Investor) ──► NDB-SS ──► National Project
                            │
                       5% Commission
                     for locking deal
```

**Model 2: Paymaster (Transaction Model)**
```
Funder ──► NDB-SS Account ──► Project Disbursements
               │                      │
         Fund Management         Transaction Fees
              Fee                   (0.5-1%)
```

**Model 3: Co-Investment**
```
NDB-SS Capital + Partner Capital ──► Project
              │
        Returns + Carried Interest
```

## 4. Strategic Sectors

### 4.1 Priority Sector: Roads & Transport Infrastructure

**Rationale:**
- Most critical infrastructure gap
- Enables all other economic activity
- High visibility, nation-building impact
- Clear revenue models (tolls, fuel levies)
- Strategic trade corridor connecting South Sudan to Uganda and East African markets

**Priority Project: Juba-Nimule Highway**

| Project | Est. Cost | Priority | Status |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Juba-Nimule Highway (Uganda border) | $400M | Critical | Pipeline |

**Project Overview:**
The Juba-Nimule Highway is a 192km road connecting South Sudan's capital to the Uganda border, representing the country's most vital trade and transport corridor. This project will:
- Reduce travel time from 8+ hours to under 3 hours
- Enable efficient import/export of goods
- Connect to the Northern Corridor (Mombasa-Kampala)
- Generate toll revenue for sustainability

| **Roads Subtotal** | **$400 million** | |

### 4.2 Priority Sector: Mining & Natural Resources

**Rationale:**
- Vast untapped gold deposits in Eastern Equatoria
- Diversification from oil dependence critical
- High-value export potential with established global markets
- Attracts serious international mining investors

**Priority Project: Gold Mining Development**

| Project | Est. Cost | Priority | Status |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Gold Mining Development (Kapoeta Region) | $300M | High | Pipeline |

**Project Overview:**
The Kapoeta gold mining region in Eastern Equatoria holds significant untapped gold reserves. This project encompasses:
- Exploration and geological surveys
- Mining infrastructure development
- Processing facilities
- Environmental compliance systems
- Community development programs

Estimated gold reserves and favorable geology make this a compelling investment opportunity for mining companies and development financiers.

| **Mining Subtotal** | **$300 million** | |

### 4.3 Priority Sector: Agro-processing

**Rationale:**
- Value addition to agricultural produce critical for economic development
- Reduces post-harvest losses (currently 40%+)
- Creates employment in processing and packaging
- Enables export of processed goods to regional markets
- Foundation for food security and import substitution

**Priority Project: Agro-processing Facilities**

| Project | Est. Cost | Priority | Status |
|---------|-----------|----------|--------|
| Agro-processing Facilities | $200M | High | Pipeline |

**Project Overview:**
Establishment of modern agro-processing facilities to add value to South Sudan's agricultural output:
- Grain milling and flour production
- Oilseed processing (sesame, groundnuts)
- Fruit and vegetable processing
- Meat processing and cold chain
- Packaging and quality control facilities

These facilities will be strategically located near agricultural production zones and transport corridors.

| **Agro-processing Subtotal** | **$200 million** | |

## 8. Risk Analysis & Mitigation

### 8.1 Key Risks

| Risk Category | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---------------|------|------------|--------|------------|
| **Political** | Instability, conflict | High | High | Diversify geography, donor partnerships |
| **Economic** | Oil price volatility | High | Medium | Focus on non-oil sectors |
| **Credit** | Project defaults | Medium | High | Rigorous due diligence, guarantees |
| **Operational** | Capacity constraints | Medium | Medium | International partnerships, training |
| **Regulatory** | Policy changes | Low | Medium | Government engagement, charter protection |
| **Reputational** | Project failures | Medium | High | Strong governance, impact monitoring |
| **FX** | Currency volatility | High | Medium | USD-denominated operations |

### 8.2 Risk Mitigation Framework

**Political Risk Mitigation:**
- Multilateral co-investment (MIGA, AfDB guarantees)
- Political risk insurance
- Diversification across regions (East Africa, Pan-African)
- Strong government relations with independence

**Credit Risk Mitigation:**
- Comprehensive due diligence
- Government guarantees where possible
- Collateral and security packages
- Phased disbursements tied to milestones

**Operational Risk Mitigation:**
- International advisory partnerships
- Robust internal controls
- Technology infrastructure
- Staff development programs
