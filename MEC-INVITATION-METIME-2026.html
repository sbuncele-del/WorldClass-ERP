Re: Invitation to be Guest Speaker and Request for Sponsorship of 10 Women Business Exhibitors
Dear Honourable MEC Lenkopane,
I am writing on behalf of the Isago Foundation to respectfully invite you to be a Guest Speaker at the upcoming MeTIME women empowerment event, scheduled for the 4th and 5th of September 2026 at Sun City, North West Province, Republic of South Africa.
MeTIME is a flagship initiative spearheaded by the Isago Foundation, focusing on advancing women in the economy, strengthening networks, and supporting women's growth, resilience, and survival in both business and community spaces.
We would be deeply honoured to have you deliver the keynote address during the Saturday evening Business Insights programme. Your distinguished leadership in driving economic growth, empowering informal traders, promoting industrialisation, and championing sustainable development in the North West Province makes you the ideal voice to inspire and empower the women in attendance. A presentation on the Department's services, programmes, and opportunities available to women entrepreneurs would be of tremendous value to our delegates.
In addition to the speaking invitation, we are also seeking the Department's support to sponsor the participation of 10 women-owned businesses in exhibition packages at the event, enabling them to attend and fully benefit from this transformative experience. The sponsorship would go toward securing branding visibility, access to market including potential leads, and overall strategic brand alignment for these women — ensuring they are not only present but actively included in high-value conversations, networking opportunities, and exposure to leadership.
We believe that representation matters deeply. Being in spaces surrounded by accomplished and empowered women can be life-changing — opening doors, shifting perspectives, and building confidence for women to thrive in their respective paths.
We would greatly appreciate the opportunity to meet with you and your office to discuss this partnership further, and to explore how we can align this initiative with the Department's goals and impact areas.
