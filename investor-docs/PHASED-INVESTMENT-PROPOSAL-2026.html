Purpose of This Document

This document presents a detailed, phased investment proposal for SiyaBusa ERP, the flagship product of Masaphokati Technologies (Pty) Ltd. It is intended for prospective investors considering a seed-stage equity position in the company.

Background

Following discussions with prospective investors, it became clear that a straightforward breakdown of how funds will be used — and when — would serve both parties better than a single lump-sum deployment. This document is the result of that feedback.

A clearer breakdown of the use of funds has been prepared to demonstrate transparency and allow for meaningful accountability at every stage. Rather than requesting the full R2,000,000 upfront, we have adopted a phased funding approach: the investor commits the total amount, but capital is released in three tranches, each unlocked only when agreed milestones are met and verified through monthly progress reports and board sign-off.

The first tranche of R700,000 covers the launch phase — enough to put the commercial engine in motion without deploying more capital than the business needs at that stage. Sometimes a new business does not need all the money at once. What it needs is the right amount at the right time, tied to real progress. That is the principle behind this proposal.

SiyaBusa ERP is a fully built, production-ready cloud ERP system — deployed on Digital Ocean, with native South African compliance (SARS, BBBEE, POPIA, BCEA). The product includes its own built-in CRM, sales pipeline, and client management tools, which means we do not need to spend on third-party CRM or sales software. The technology risk is zero. What this investment buys is customers and commercial momentum.

The Phased Investment Rationale

Why Phase the Investment?

Phased funding creates accountability at every stage. Capital is released only when milestones are demonstrated, not promised. The business builds itself — revenue from Month 4 funds recurring costs, and the seed capital is an accelerant, not life support.

For the Investor

  • Capital released only when milestones are verified
  • Phase 1 (R700K) proves the model before R1.3M more is committed
  • Monthly 1-page progress reports — full pipeline transparency
  • If Phase 1 underperforms, Phase 2 is restructured before deployment
  • Board observer seat — active visibility, not passive hope

For the Business

  • Forces disciplined execution — every rand must earn the next tranche
  • Sales consulting model: accountability without HR risk
  • Company vehicle owned outright — capital asset, no monthly lease drain
  • Business becomes self-funding from Month 9 (EBITDA positive)
  • Seed capital is the accelerant; revenue is the engine

Company Vehicle — Outright Purchase Justification

Why outright purchase beats an operating lease: An operating lease drains R8,500/month indefinitely with nothing to show. An outright purchase of R185,000 creates a company asset on the balance sheet — improving the company's net asset value for future funding rounds, fully deductible via SARS Section 11(e) wear-and-tear (20% per year), and eliminates all monthly lease obligations. This is the prudent commercial decision.
