## 4. PROJECT FULL DESCRIPTION

### 4.1. PURPOSE

> The purpose of this project is to establish a sustainable cattle feedlot operation at Lushikishini to:
> 
> 1. **Generate income** for 10 community members and their families through commercial beef production
> 2. **Create employment** opportunities in cattle farming and management
> 3. **Contribute to food security** by producing quality beef for the local market
> 4. **Develop agricultural skills** among youth and community members
> 5. **Demonstrate** the viability of feedlot farming as a rural enterprise model
> 
> The project will construct 4 feedlots (3 new + 1 existing) with capacity for 40 cattle. We will purchase weaner calves, finish them over 90-120 days, and sell to abattoirs for profit. The rotational model allows us to sell 10 cattle every month for consistent income.

### 4.2. BACKGROUND OF COMMUNITY (PROBLEMS)

> The Lushikishini community under Chief Somtsewu faces several challenges:
> 
> 1. **Limited employment opportunities** - Most residents depend on subsistence farming with little cash income
> 2. **Youth unemployment** - Young people leave the community seeking work in urban areas
> 3. **Underutilized resources** - The community has access to land, water, and crop residues suitable for cattle farming, but lacks capital to commercialize
> 4. **Traditional cattle farming** is slow (2-3 years to sell) and generates unpredictable income
> 5. **Poverty** - Families struggle to meet basic needs like school fees, healthcare, and household expenses
> 
> This project addresses these problems by creating a profitable agricultural enterprise that generates monthly income using locally available resources.

### 4.3. TARGET POPULATION

> **Direct Beneficiaries:**
> - **10 members** (cooperative members who will share profits equally)
>   - Males: 8
>   - Females: 2
> 
> **Indirect Beneficiaries:**
> - **50+ dependents** (family members of the 10 cooperative members)
> - **Wider community** - through skills transfer, employment of casual labor, and purchase of local feed/materials
> 
> **Total beneficiaries:** Approximately 60+ people

### 4.4. IMPACT (CHANGE AS A RESULT OF THE PROJECT)

> **Economic Impact:**
> - Each member will earn approximately **E4,500 - E5,500 per month** (E54,000+ annually)
> - Combined annual income for all members: **E540,000+**
> - Reduced poverty and improved living standards for 10 households
> 
> **Social Impact:**
> - Youth retention in the community (alternative to urban migration)
> - Skills development in cattle management, business, and financial literacy
> - Model for other communities to replicate
> - Strengthened community cooperation and unity
> 
> **Agricultural Impact:**
> - Productive use of crop residues (reduces waste)
> - Introduction of modern cattle finishing techniques
> - Contribution to local beef supply
> - Manure production for organic fertilizer
> 
> **Long-term Impact:**
> - Sustainable income generation beyond the grant period
> - Potential for expansion and growth
> - Transfer of knowledge to next generation
