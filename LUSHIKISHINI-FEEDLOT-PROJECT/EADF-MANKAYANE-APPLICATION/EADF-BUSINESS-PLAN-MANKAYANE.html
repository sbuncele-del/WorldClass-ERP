2.2 What I Am Applying For

COMPONENT 1: Feed Storage Facility — E550,000

The Problem: Feed must be purchased frequently in small quantities at retail prices. Without proper storage, feed is exposed to rain, moisture, rodents, and insects, causing spoilage losses of 15–20%. I cannot take advantage of seasonal bulk pricing.

The Solution: A purpose-built 300 m² agricultural storage facility.

FeatureSpecification
TypeSteel-frame agricultural storage building
Size20m × 15m = 300 m²
Height4.5m to eaves
FoundationReinforced concrete slab (150mm)
Walls & RoofIBR corrugated steel sheeting (0.5mm galvanized)
Doors2 × double sliding doors (3m wide) for vehicle access
VentilationRidge vents + side louvers to prevent moisture buildup
FloorSealed concrete — rodent-proof
ElectricalLED lighting, power points for feed machine
Capacity80–100 tonnes of feed (3–4 months supply)

Benefits:

  • Eliminate 15–20% feed spoilage → saves E3,000–E5,000/month
  • Bulk purchasing at 20–30% discount → saves E5,000–E7,000/month
  • Seasonal purchasing when prices are lowest
  • Secure storage for equipment, medicines, and supplies
  • Houses the feed production machine (Component 3)
  • Permanent asset with 30+ year lifespan

COMPONENT 2: Breeding Cattle — E150,000

The Problem: All cattle must be purchased as weaners from the market at E6,000–E7,500 per head. Prices fluctuate, supply is not guaranteed, and this is a significant ongoing cash outflow.

The Solution: Purchase 10 breeding cows and 1 quality breeding bull.

COMPONENT 3: Feed Production Machine — E100,000

The Problem: Commercial cattle feed concentrate costs E15–E20 per animal per day. For 30–40 cattle, feed is the single largest expense (E13,500–E24,000/month). Buying ready-made feed means paying for processing, packaging, transport, and retailer margins.

The Solution: A hammer mill and feed mixer to produce own cattle feed on-site.

7. FMD MANAGEMENT — MANKAYANE AREA

The Mankayane area was previously an FMD-affected zone. The situation has substantially improved, with movement restrictions progressively eased and vaccination campaigns completed.

My FMD Protocol:

  1. All cattle vaccinated against FMD upon arrival
  2. Movement permits obtained for all cattle movements
  3. Strict compliance with veterinary cordon regulations
  4. Regular inspections by Department of Veterinary Services
  5. On-site isolation/quarantine area
  6. Daily health monitoring and immediate reporting
  7. Full cooperation with all government disease control measures
