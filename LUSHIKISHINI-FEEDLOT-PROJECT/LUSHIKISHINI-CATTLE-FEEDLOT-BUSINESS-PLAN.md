# LUSHIKISHINI CATTLE FEEDLOT PROJECT
## Business Plan for Regional Development Fund Grant Application

---

## **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**

**Project Name:** Lushikishini Community Cattle Feedlot Project  
**Location:** Lushikishini, kaMavuso, Phondo Inkhundla, under Chief Somtsewu, Eswatini  
**Legal Structure:** Community Cooperative (10 Members)  
**Grant Requested:** E500,000 (Eswatini Lilangeni / R500,000)  
**Own Contribution (10%):** E50,000 (4 cattle valued at E40,000 + existing kraal valued at E10,000)  

**Project Summary:**  
The Lushikishini Cattle Feedlot Project is a community-driven agricultural initiative aimed at establishing a sustainable beef cattle finishing operation. The project will construct and operate 4 feedlots, each with a capacity of 10 cattle (40 cattle total), using a rotational sales model to generate consistent monthly income for 10 community members.

**Key Highlights:**
- ✅ 10 committed community members from Lushikishini
- ✅ 1 feedlot already constructed (10x12m kraal)
- ✅ 4 cattle already owned (10% contribution met)
- ✅ Access to water and grazing land
- ✅ Established abattoir relationships (buyers collect cattle)
- ✅ Strong team with agricultural, environmental, and financial expertise

---

## **1. PROJECT BACKGROUND & JUSTIFICATION**

### 1.1 The Opportunity
Eswatini's beef market presents significant opportunities for small-scale producers. The country imports substantial quantities of beef to meet domestic demand, creating a gap that local producers can fill. Cattle feedlot operations offer:

- **Higher profit margins** compared to traditional extensive cattle farming
- **Faster turnover** (90-120 day cycles vs. 2-3 years for traditional methods)
- **Predictable income** through planned sales cycles
- **Value addition** by finishing cattle to market-ready weights

### 1.2 Community Need
The Lushikishini community, like many rural areas in Eswatini, faces:
- Limited formal employment opportunities
- Underutilization of available agricultural resources
- Youth unemployment and migration to urban areas
- Need for sustainable income-generating activities

### 1.3 Why Feedlot Farming?
| Traditional Cattle Farming | Feedlot Finishing |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| 2-3 years to market weight | 90-120 days to market weight |
| Unpredictable income | Monthly sales cycle |
| Weather-dependent | Controlled feeding environment |
| Lower weight gains (0.3-0.5 kg/day) | Higher weight gains (0.8-1.2 kg/day) |
| Variable meat quality | Consistent quality for premium prices |

## **2. PROJECT DESCRIPTION**

### 2.1 Business Model
The project operates on a **batch feeding and rotational sales model**:

1. **Purchase** weaner calves (180-220 kg) at market prices
2. **Feed** intensively for 90-120 days using crop residues and supplementary feed
3. **Sell** finished cattle (380-450 kg) to abattoirs
4. **Repeat** cycle with new batch

### 2.2 Infrastructure Plan

| Feedlot | Status | Capacity | Construction Cost |
|---------|--------|----------|-------------------|
| Feedlot 1 | ✅ Built | 10 cattle | E10,000 (own contribution) |
| Feedlot 2 | To be built | 10 cattle | E35,000 |
| Feedlot 3 | To be built | 10 cattle | E35,000 |
| Feedlot 4 | To be built | 10 cattle | E35,000 |
| **Total** | | **40 cattle** | **E115,000** |

### 2.3 Feedlot Specifications
Each feedlot (kraal) will be:
- **Size:** 10m x 12m (120 m²) = 12 m² per animal (industry standard: 10-15 m²)
- **Materials:** Treated gum poles, wire mesh, concrete water troughs
- **Features:** 
  - Feeding troughs (concrete or steel)
  - Water supply connection
  - Drainage system
  - Handling crush for veterinary care

## **4. OPERATIONS PLAN**

### 4.1 Production Cycle (Per Feedlot of 10 Cattle)

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Week 1 | Purchase 10 weaner calves (avg 200 kg) |
| Week 1-2 | Adaptation period (gradual feed transition) |
| Week 2-12 | Intensive feeding phase |
| Week 12-14 | Finishing phase (increase energy in diet) |
| Week 14-16 | Marketing and sale to abattoir |
| Week 17 | Rest period, kraal cleaning, new batch purchase |

### 4.2 Feeding Program

**Feed Sources:**
1. **Crop residues** (primary) - maize stover, grass hay, legume residues
2. **Supplementary feed** - commercial cattle concentrate or homemade mix
3. **Mineral licks** - for balanced nutrition

**Daily Feed Requirements (per animal):**
| Feed Type | Amount | Cost/day | Monthly Cost (10 cattle) |
|-----------|--------|----------|-------------------------|
| Roughage (crop residues) | 8-10 kg | E5 | E1,500 |
| Concentrate | 3-4 kg | E15 | E4,500 |
| Minerals | 50g | E1 | E300 |
| **Total** | | **E21/day** | **E6,300/month** |

### 4.3 Animal Health Management
- **Vaccinations:** Lumpy skin disease, Blackleg, Anthrax (as required)
- **Deworming:** Entry and mid-cycle
- **Dipping:** Regular tick control
- **Daily observation:** Early disease detection
- **Veterinary support:** Access to government veterinary services

### 4.4 Stocking Schedule (Full Operation)

| Month | Feedlot 1 | Feedlot 2 | Feedlot 3 | Feedlot 4 | Sales |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Month 1 | Buy 10 | - | - | - | - |
| Month 2 | Feeding | Buy 10 | - | - | - |
| Month 3 | Feeding | Feeding | Buy 10 | - | - |
| Month 4 | Sell 10 | Feeding | Feeding | Buy 10 | 10 cattle |
| Month 5 | Buy 10 | Sell 10 | Feeding | Feeding | 10 cattle |
| Month 6 | Feeding | Buy 10 | Sell 10 | Feeding | 10 cattle |
| Month 7+ | **Continuous rotation - selling 10 cattle per month** |

## **7. RISK ANALYSIS & MITIGATION**

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|------------|--------|---------------------|
| **Cattle disease outbreak** | Medium | High | Vaccination program, quarantine protocols, veterinary relationship |
| **Feed price increases** | Medium | Medium | Bulk purchasing, grow own fodder, diversify feed sources |
| **Cattle theft** | Low | High | Security fencing, night watch rotation, community awareness |
| **Market price drop** | Medium | Medium | Maintain quality for premium prices, build buyer relationships |
| **Drought affecting feed** | Medium | Medium | Feed storage, crop residue agreements with farmers |
| **Member conflict** | Low | Medium | Clear constitution, regular meetings, transparent finances |
