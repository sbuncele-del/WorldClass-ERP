# Understanding SAFE - Simple Agreement for Future Equity

## In Plain English (Layman's Terms)

### What is a SAFE?

**SAFE = "I give you money now, you give me shares later"**

Think of it like this:

> Imagine you're opening a restaurant. Your friend believes in you and gives you R100,000 to help you start. But instead of lending you money (which you'd have to pay back with interest), your friend says: *"When your restaurant becomes successful and you bring in bigger investors, give me shares at a discount."*

That's essentially what a SAFE is.

### Why SAFE Instead of a Loan?

| Loan | SAFE |
|:-----|:-----|
| Must be repaid with interest | No repayment - converts to ownership |
| Monthly payments stress cash flow | No payments until exit |
| If business fails, still owe money | If business fails, investor loses (like you) |
| Doesn't align investor with success | Investor wins when you win |

---

### Why SAFE Instead of Selling Shares Now?

| Selling Shares Now | SAFE |
|:-------------------|:-----|
| Need expensive lawyer to value company | Simple document, low legal costs |
| Need to agree on exact valuation | Defer valuation to later |
| Takes months to negotiate | Can close in days |
| Creates complex shareholder structure | Clean cap table until conversion |

---

### The Key Terms Explained

#### 1. Valuation Cap (R15,000,000)

**Plain English:** *"No matter how valuable the company becomes, your investment converts as if the company is worth R15M."*

**Example:**
- Investor puts in R2,850,000
- Later, company valued at R100,000,000
- Without cap: R2,850,000 ÷ R100M = 2.85% ownership
- With R15M cap: R2,850,000 ÷ R15M = 19% ownership

**The cap protects the early investor for taking risk when the company was unproven.**

#### 2. Discount (20%)

**Plain English:** *"Whatever price new investors pay, you pay 20% less."*

**Example:**
- New investor buys shares at R100 each
- SAFE investor pays R80 each (20% discount)
- Same money buys 25% more shares

#### 3. Which Applies?

**The investor gets whichever method gives them MORE shares.** Usually the valuation cap is better if the company grows significantly.

### When Does the Investor Get Their Money Back?

**They don't get "money back" - they get shares that become valuable.**

```
EXIT SCENARIOS (How Investor Makes Money)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SCENARIO 1: ACQUISITION
────────────────────────
A big company (e.g., Sage, Microsoft) buys SiyaBusa for R200M.
Investor's shares = 19% of R200M = R38M
Return: R38M on R2.85M investment = 13x return

SCENARIO 2: IPO (Stock Market Listing)
──────────────────────────────────────
SiyaBusa lists on JSE at R500M valuation.
Investor's shares = 19% of R500M = R95M
(Can sell shares on open market)

SCENARIO 3: SECONDARY SALE
─────────────────────────
New investor in Series B wants to buy some existing shares.
Early investor can sell some/all shares to them.

SCENARIO 4: DIVIDENDS
────────────────────
If company becomes profitable and pays dividends,
investor receives proportional dividends.

SCENARIO 5: COMPANY FAILS
────────────────────────
Investor loses their investment (just like founders lose their time).
This is the risk they took.
```

### Risks for the Investor (Be Honest About These)

| Risk | Explanation |
|:-----|:------------|
| **Company fails** | Investor loses entire R2.85M |
| **No exit happens** | Shares exist but can't be sold |
| **Dilution** | Future funding rounds reduce percentage |
| **Long wait** | May take 5-10 years to see returns |
| **Illiquid** | Can't easily sell shares like stock market |

---

### Why This is Fair for Both Sides

**For You (Founder):**
- No debt to repay
- No monthly payments
- Investor is aligned with your success
- Simple, fast to close

**For Investor:**
- Protected by valuation cap
- Gets rewarded for early risk
- Upside if company succeeds
- Standard instrument (not unusual)

---

### How to Explain It in 30 Seconds

> *"I'm raising R2.85M using a SAFE - it's like a promise to give shares later. You're not lending me money, you're buying a ticket to own part of the company. The R15M cap means even if we're worth R100M later, your shares are calculated as if we're worth R15M - so you get rewarded for believing early. You make money when we exit - either someone buys us, or we list on the stock market."*

## Questions Investors Might Ask

**Q: Is this a loan?**
A: No. You're buying future equity, not lending money.

**Q: When do I get my money back?**
A: You don't get money "back" - your investment converts to shares, which become valuable at exit.

**Q: What if there's no exit?**
A: Your shares exist but may be hard to sell. This is the risk of early-stage investing.

**Q: What if the company fails?**
A: You lose your investment. This is why SAFEs are for risk-tolerant investors.

**Q: Can I get my money out early?**
A: Not automatically. You'd need to find a buyer for your SAFE or shares.
