Section 02
Problem Definition

The South African SME and mid-market business sector operates in one of the most demanding regulatory and operational environments in the world — yet the tools available to them are either inadequate, foreign-built, or prohibitively expensive.

The Core Problem: A Four-Part Failure

1

Cost Exclusion

  • SAP Business One: R180,000–R600,000/year for mid-market deployment
  • Sage 300: R50,000–R200,000/year + implementation costs
  • Oracle NetSuite: USD $30,000–$100,000/year minimum
  • Most SA SMEs and growing businesses cannot afford these solutions
2

Regulatory Blindness

  • Foreign ERP systems are not built for SARS eFiling, PAYE, UIF, SDL compliance
  • BBBEE scorecard management is absent or bolted on as afterthought
  • South African labour law (BCEA, LRA) nuances are not natively supported
  • Businesses spend R5,000–R40,000/month extra on compliance consultants
3

Fragmentation

  • Most businesses run 5–15 disconnected tools: accounting, payroll, CRM, inventory, HR separately
  • Data silos lead to errors, duplicated work, and poor decision-making
  • Integration costs between tools consume 15–25% of operational IT budgets
4

Foreign Infrastructure Risk

  • Data sovereignty concerns — sensitive business data hosted abroad
  • Latency issues from overseas servers affecting daily operations
  • Currency risk on USD/EUR-priced software with rand depreciation
  • No local support structure or understanding of SA business context
