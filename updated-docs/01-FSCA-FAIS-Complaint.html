1.1   I am an individual investor who invested a total of R2,175,375.00 (Two Million One Hundred and Seventy-Five Thousand Three Hundred and Seventy-Five Rand) over a two-year period (2020 and 2021) through Impact Empowerment Ventures (Pty) Ltd ("IEV"), a Venture Capital Company ("VCC") approved by the South African Revenue Service ("SARS") in terms of Section 12J of the Income Tax Act, No. 58 of 1962.

1.3   My funds were invested by IEV into The Joseph Company / Muma Investments for the purposes of Class D Student Accommodation, as a qualifying investment under Section 12J.

1.4   The qualifying shares in The Joseph Company were issued to Impact Empowerment Ventures (the VCC) in its name. I was issued only an Investor Certificate by IEV — not direct shares in the qualifying company.

1.5   As per the Section 12J rules, the investment was subject to a 5-year restriction period, after which the investment should become accessible to me, subject to performance and valuation.

2. Conduct Complained Of

2.1 Failure to Provide Information

2.1.1   My earliest investment (Investor No. S12J IIM 2019/147, Share Certificate D4) reached its 5-year maturity in approximately 2025.

2.1.2   Since June 2025 (approximately 9 months ago), I have repeatedly requested the following from IEV:

  1. SARS interim statements for all three investments;
  2. Investment performance report for the matured investment;
  3. Guidance on the withdrawal process post the prescribed lock-in duration.

2.1.3   Despite numerous written follow-ups (18 June 2025, 26 June 2025, 14 July 2025, 12 August 2025, 19 February 2026, and 20 February 2026), I have received only vague assurances that the information is "under review" or "being finalised."

2.1.4   At no point has IEV provided me with:

  • Any investment performance figures;
  • Any valuation of my investment;
  • Any SARS interim statements; or
  • Any concrete withdrawal procedure.

2.2 Suspected Engineered Liquidation

2.2.1   IEV has been placed in formal liquidation, with Marumo Trustees appointed as liquidator.

2.2.2   I have reason to believe that this liquidation may have been engineered or orchestrated to avoid obligations to investors. My basis for this suspicion is:

  1. The debts that IEV allegedly could not pay were, to my knowledge, minor in nature and disproportionate to the scale of investor funds under management;
  2. The underlying investment in The Joseph Company / Muma Investments is reportedly performing well — its Group Chairman, Mr Dandy Matamala, confirmed receipt of the invested funds;
  3. Despite the underlying assets performing, IEV claims it cannot meet its obligations;
  4. The timing and circumstances of the liquidation raise questions about whether it was a deliberate mechanism to strip investors of their claims.

2.3 Structural Prejudice to Investors

2.3.1   The qualifying shares in The Joseph Company are registered in IEV's name, not in the names of individual investors. Investors like myself hold only Investor Certificates.

2.3.2   As a result of IEV's liquidation, my investment is now trapped in the liquidation estate, and I am at risk of being treated as an unsecured creditor rather than the beneficial owner of the underlying investment.

2.3.3   This structural arrangement may constitute a failure by IEV to adequately protect investor interests, in possible contravention of its duties as a financial services provider.

2.4 Communication Failures

2.4.1   IEV has failed to provide timely, transparent, and accurate communication to investors regarding:

  • The financial status of their investments;
  • The reasons for and circumstances of the liquidation;
  • The implications for investors' funds and Section 12J tax positions.

3. Regulatory and Legal Grounds

3.1   This complaint is lodged under the following legislative and regulatory frameworks:

  • Financial Advisory and Intermediary Services Act, No. 37 of 2002 (FAIS Act) — IEV, as FSP No. 49377, owed duties of care, skill, and diligence to its clients/investors;
  • General Code of Conduct for Authorised Financial Services Providers — requiring honest, fair, and transparent dealing;
  • Section 12J of the Income Tax Act — the regulatory framework under which IEV operated as an approved VCC;
  • Companies Act, No. 71 of 2008 — duties of directors, reckless trading, and potential mismanagement;
  • The common law — fiduciary duties owed to investors.

3.2   I respectfully request that the FSCA investigate whether:

  1. IEV was properly licensed and compliant;
  2. IEV's directors acted in the best interests of investors;
  3. The liquidation was engineered to prejudice investors;
  4. IEV's failure to provide information constitutes a contravention of the FAIS Act and/or the General Code of Conduct.

4. Relief Sought

4.1   I respectfully request the FSCA to:

  1. Investigate the conduct of Impact Empowerment Ventures (Pty) Ltd and its directors;
  2. Determine whether the liquidation was engineered to the detriment of Section 12J investors;
  3. Intervene to protect the interests of investors whose funds are trapped in the liquidation estate;
  4. Direct the liquidator to provide full transparency regarding the status of investor funds and the underlying qualifying investments;
  5. Consider enforcement action against the responsible parties if misconduct is established;
  6. Advise on the recourse available to affected investors.
