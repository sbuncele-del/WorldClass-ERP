1. Purpose of This Document

This document has been prepared by Masaphokati Equity Holdings to present, for discussion purposes, the corporate structure, asset base, and proposed investment framework surrounding SiyaBusa ERP. It was prompted by conversations with prospective strategic partners but is addressed broadly to any investor or partner who may be considering involvement.

What is presented here is a picture of what has been built, how the company is structured, and the framework being considered for future investment — so that any interested party can assess whether there is alignment before formal proceedings begin.

A Framework Is Being Proposed — Not Yet Offered
Masaphokati Technologies has developed a complete investor documentation framework: Business Plan, Term Sheet, SAFE Agreement, Subscription Agreement, Shareholders Agreement, and a plain-English SAFE guide. These documents exist as a proposed framework. The structures described herein are subject to further discussion, legal review, and board approval before any formal offer is made.

4. Two Proposed Participation Paths — For Discussion

The proposed investment framework under consideration contemplates two participation paths, drawn from the documentation already prepared. These are presented for discussion purposes. If there is alignment in principle, formal documentation would be progressed with legal advisers on both sides.

Path A — SAFE Agreement (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)

Under consideration for investors who wish to participate early, with conversion to shares deferred to the next priced round.

SAFE Agreement — Key Terms
Valuation CapR15,000,000
Discount on Next Round20% (investor pays 80% of the next round price)
Conversion TriggerNext qualifying equity financing of R5,000,000+
Minimum InvestmentR30,000
Investor Rights NowMonthly reports, Pro Rata rights, Board observer (10%+), MFN clause
Shares IssuedNot now — converts to shares at qualifying event (IPO, acquisition, or Series A)
Conversion MethodCalculationExample — R500,000 Invested
Cap Method
Valuation Cap ÷ Total Shares		 Price = R15M ÷ shares outstanding at conversion At 120M shares: R15M ÷ 120M = R0.125/share
R500K ÷ R0.125 = 4,000,000 shares
Discount Method
80% of Next Round Price		 Price = next round price × 80% If next round prices at R0.20: investor pays R0.16
R500K ÷ R0.16 = 3,125,000 shares
Investor Always Gets the Better of the Two
The SAFE converts at whichever method produces more shares. In this example: 4,000,000 shares (Cap Method) beats 3,125,000 (Discount Method) — the investor gets 4M shares. The early investor who takes the most risk gets the most reward.

9. Proposed Next Steps if There Is Alignment

  1. Initial conversation — An introductory discussion to establish whether there is alignment on vision, structure, and terms. No commitments made at this stage.
  2. Review of full investor documentation — The complete Business Plan, Term Sheet, SAFE Explained, and draft agreements are available to serious prospective partners under a Non-Disclosure Agreement.
  3. Expression of interest — If, after reviewing the documentation, there is continued interest, the prospective investor completes the non-binding expression of interest (Section 6 above).
  4. Due diligence — Masaphokati Technologies will make available relevant technical, financial, and legal information for independent due diligence by the investor.
  5. Legal documentation — If both parties wish to proceed, commercial attorneys on both sides draft and review the relevant agreements (SAFE or Subscription Agreement, Shareholders Agreement).
  6. Formal execution — Agreements executed, funds transferred, cap table updated, and investor reporting commences.
