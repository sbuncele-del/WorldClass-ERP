4. QuickBooks System Errors — Acknowledgement

During the preparation of the 2025 financial statements, we discovered material system errors within the QuickBooks accounting platform that had affected the integrity of the 2024 records. These errors were not apparent during the initial 2024 year-end process as they were hidden within the system's internal processing.

We have since raised these issues directly with Intuit (QuickBooks), who have acknowledged the errors and have confirmed that they are currently working on resolving them. As a consequence of these system-level issues, we took the decision to recapture the accounting records from scratch onto a new, reliable platform to ensure data integrity and to prevent any recurrence of these errors going forward.

Impact: The QuickBooks system errors contributed to the misstatements identified in the 2024 comparative figures. The full recapture exercise has been completed and all 2025 figures presented herein are accurate and verified.

  • Gross Profit: R4,455,918 (13.3% margin)
  • Operating Expenses: (R400,562)

The entity generated substantial revenue during the 2025 financial year. It is important to note that the majority of income was absorbed by cost of sales (86.7% of revenue), which is consistent with the nature of an engineering and contracting business where direct project costs constitute the bulk of expenditure. This explains the relatively high profit figure — the entity maintained lean operating expenses (only R400,562), demonstrating disciplined cost management.

Furthermore, rather than deploying surplus cash into discretionary expenditure, management has adopted the prudent approach of retaining funds as liquid cash and short-term investments (R6,400,000 in call deposits and R9,480,413 in bank accounts). This is regarded as good financial practice, ensuring the entity maintains a strong liquidity position and is well-placed to meet future obligations.
